Monty Hall

The Monty Hall or colloquially "goat" problem is a probability puzzle named after Monty Hall and his TV-show "Let's Make a Deal". The Monty Hall problem is intellectually intriguing for its counterintuitive underlying probabilities. The problem can be described as follows:

Suppose you are on a game show, and you are given the choice of three doors: Behind one door is a car. Behind the others are goats. You pick a door, say door 1, and the host, who knows what is behind the doors, opens another door, say door 3, which has a goat. He then says to you, "Do you want to pick door 2?" Is it to your advantage to switch your choice?

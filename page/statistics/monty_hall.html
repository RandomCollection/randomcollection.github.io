Monty Hall

The Monty Hall or colloquially "goat" problem is a probability puzzle named after Monty Hall and his TV-show "Let's Make a Deal". The Monty Hall problem is intellectually intriguing for its counterintuitive underlying probabilities. The problem can be described as follows:

Suppose you are on the game show "Let's Make a Deal" and you are given the choice of three doors: Behind one door is a car, the prize. Behind the other two doors are goats, the blanks. You pick a door, say door 1, and Monty, who knows what is behind each door, opens another door, say door 3, which has a goat. Monty then asks you whether you want to stay with your selected door or switch to the other one. Is it to your advantage to switch your choice?
